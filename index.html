Electoral rights

Know your rights to accessible voting, secrecy of ballot, and assistance for voters with special needs.

Step 1: Register to vote Now to early deadline

Confirm your eligibility, submit registration online or in person, and verify your details before the cutoff date. This is the first essential step.

Step 2: Learn the candidates Weeks before election

Review candidate platforms, attend forums, and compare policies. Good preparation helps you make an informed decision on voting day.

Step 3: Prepare to vote Days before election

Check your polling location, find sample ballots, and gather IDs or documents needed. Arrive early and know your options for early or absentee voting.

Step 4: Vote confidently Election day

Follow instructions at the polling place, review your selections, and submit your ballot. If you need help, poll workers are there to assist.

Step 5: Track results After polls close

Watch official updates, verify outcomes from trusted sources, and understand the next steps for certification and transition.

How do I know if I can vote?

Eligibility usually depends on age, citizenship, and residency. Check local election websites for the exact qualifications in your area.

What happens after I vote?

Your ballot is counted and results are certified. Follow official news sources and election authorities for accurate updates.

Can I vote early?

Many regions offer early voting and absentee ballots. Find your local deadlines and procedures before the election day.

Who can help at the polling place?

Poll workers are there to assist with questions, accessibility needs, and ballot instructions. Ask politely if you need guidance.
